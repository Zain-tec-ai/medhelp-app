# MedHelp Content Guidelines

## Overview
MedHelp provides educational health information for symptom awareness, medicine reference, and condition understanding. All content must be reviewed by medical professionals.

## Content Categories

### Symptoms
- Brief, clear description of the symptom
- Common causes (non-exhaustive)
- When to seek emergency care
- Self-care suggestions
- Do NOT diagnose

**Example:**
```
Fever: A temporary rise in body temperature usually indicating the body is fighting infection.
- Common causes: Flu, cold, infection, vaccination
- Seek emergency care if: Temperature >104°F, severe symptoms, difficulty breathing
- Self-care: Rest, hydration, cool compress
```

### Medicines
- Common name and medical name
- What it's used for
- How it works (simplified)
- Common side effects
- Important safety notes (pregnancy, interactions, etc.)
- Do NOT advise on dosage

**Example:**
```
Paracetamol (Acetaminophen): Pain reliever and fever reducer.
- Used for: Headache, muscle pain, fever
- How it works: Reduces pain signals and lowers body temperature
- Common side effects: Stomach upset (rare at recommended doses)
- Safety: Avoid combining with other paracetamol-containing products. Not recommended in severe liver disease.
```

### Conditions
- What the condition is
- How it develops and risk factors
- Typical symptoms
- General management approaches
- When to seek care
- Do NOT promise cures

**Example:**
```
Type 2 Diabetes: A chronic condition where the body cannot effectively use or produce insulin.
- Development: Often linked to lifestyle, genetics, age
- Symptoms: Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue
- Management: Diet, exercise, medication, monitoring
- Seek care if: New or worsening symptoms, difficulty controlling blood sugar
```

## Medical Review Requirements

1. **All content must be reviewed** by licensed medical professionals in the relevant field
2. **Review checklist:**
   - [ ] Medically accurate
   - [ ] Age-appropriate (if specified)
   - [ ] Not giving specific medical advice
   - [ ] Appropriate emergency warnings included
   - [ ] No marketing or promotional claims

3. **Reviewer credentials:**
   - Medical Doctor (MD) or Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO)
   - Nurse Practitioner (NP) or Physician Assistant (PA) for selected content
   - Pharmacist for medication content
   - Specialists for condition-specific content

## What NOT to Include

❌ Specific dosage recommendations
❌ Personal medical advice
❌ Diagnoses
❌ Guarantees of cure
❌ Unproven treatments
❌ Marketing or advertising
❌ Medical device promotions
❌ Unlicensed practitioner advice

## What TO Include

✓ Educational information
✓ General wellness tips
✓ Emergency warning signs
✓ Reliable resource links
✓ Encouragement to see healthcare provider
✓ Diverse, inclusive language
✓ Plain language explanations

## Adding New Content

1. **Create content** using the category templates above
2. **Request review** from appropriate medical professional
3. **Update database** with reviewed_by and reviewed_at fields
4. **Set created_at** to current timestamp
5. **Test search** to ensure keywords work

## Search Optimization

Keywords should include:
- Common names and alternate names
- Related symptoms
- Related conditions
- Related medicines
- Lay terms

**Example for "Hypertension":**
```
keywords: "high blood pressure, hypertension, BP, elevated, cardiovascular, heart, stroke, kidney"
```

## Disclaimer

Every health topic page should include:

> **Medical Disclaimer**: This information is educational only and not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider for medical concerns. In emergencies, call your local emergency number.
